# Code of Conduct

PwrAgent is built around a simple standard: do good work, add value, and treat
other contributors like capable adults.

## Expected Behavior

- Keep discussions focused on the project, the code, the docs, and the user
  outcome.
- Be direct, specific, and constructive when disagreeing.
- Back technical claims with evidence, examples, tests, or clear reasoning.
- Accept review in the same spirit: the goal is better software.
- Respect maintainer decisions about project direction, scope, and quality bar.
- Help keep issues and pull requests actionable.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Personal attacks, threats, or sustained hostility.
- Derailing technical discussions into unrelated arguments.
- Spamming, trolling, or repeatedly ignoring maintainer direction.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Submitting or encouraging malicious, deceptive, or intentionally harmful
  changes.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, issues, pull requests,
commits, wiki edits, or other contributions that do not meet this standard.
Maintainers may also limit participation when someone repeatedly makes the
project harder to build, review, use, or maintain.

Serious concerns can be reported to security@pwrdrvr.com. Reports should
include enough detail for maintainers to understand what happened and decide on
an appropriate response.
